# Energy Management

## Question 1: Activities that make me relax – Calm Quadrant

- Listening to soft music helps me feel calm and relaxed.  
- Taking a short walk outside refreshes my mind.  
- Sitting quietly without using my phone gives me mental peace.  
- Light stretching or yoga relaxes my body and mind.  
- Talking with close friends or family makes me feel supported.  
- Watching something light or funny improves my mood.  
- Practicing deep breathing reduces stress and tension.  

---

## Question 2: When do I enter the Stress Quadrant?

- I feel stressed when learning a new tool or skill.  
- Tight work deadlines increase my pressure.  
- Handling many tasks at once makes me overwhelmed.  
- Interviews or presentations create nervousness.  
- Facing unfamiliar situations makes me uncomfortable.  
- High expectations increase my stress levels.  

---

## Question 3: How do I know I am in the Excitement Quadrant?

- I feel motivated and energetic.  
- I enjoy what I am doing.  
- Time passes quickly.  
- I feel curious and interested.  
- I want to continue the activity.  
- I feel confident and positive.  

---

## Question 4: Paraphrase of “Sleep is Your Superpower” (Brief)

- Sleep improves memory and learning ability.  
- It restores brain and body health.  
- Lack of sleep affects mood and emotions.  
- Sleep strengthens immunity.  
- It reduces the risk of diseases.  
- Deep sleep clears brain toxins.  
- Sleep improves focus and productivity.  
- Dreams support emotional processing.  

---

## Question 5: Ideas to Sleep Better

- Sleep and wake up at the same time daily.  
- Avoid screens before bedtime.  
- Reduce caffeine intake at night.  
- Keep the room dark and cool.  
- Eat a light dinner.  
- Practice relaxation before sleep.  
- Get sunlight exposure in the morning.  
- Exercise regularly.  

---

## Question 6: Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise

- Exercise improves brain function.  
- It increases memory and learning ability.  
- It reduces stress and anxiety.  
- It boosts mood-enhancing chemicals.  
- It improves focus and attention.  
- It protects the brain from aging.  
- It enhances creativity.  
- It improves sleep quality.  

---

## Question 7: Steps I Can Take to Exercise More

- Walk daily for 20–30 minutes.  
- Use stairs instead of the lift.  
- Do short home workouts.  
- Stretch in the morning.  
- Play a sport weekly.  
- Exercise with friends.  
- Set small fitness goals.  
- Maintain a fixed exercise schedule.
