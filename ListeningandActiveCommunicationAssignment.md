
# Listening and Active Communication Assignment

## Question 1: Steps / Strategies for Active Listening
1. Give full attention to the speaker and avoid distractions.
2. Maintain eye contact and positive body language.
3. Do not interrupt while the other person is speaking.
4. Listen to understand, not just to reply.
5. Ask clarifying questions when something is not clear.
6. Summarize or repeat key points to confirm understanding.
7. Show interest using small responses like “yes”, “I understand”.
8. Observe tone, emotions, and body language of the speaker.

## Question 2: Key Points of Reflective Listening (Fisher’s Model)
1. Carefully listen to the full message.
2. Understand the speaker’s feelings and meaning.
3. Repeat or paraphrase what the speaker said in your own words.
4. Confirm with the speaker whether your understanding is correct.
5. Show empathy and understanding while responding.

## Question 3: Obstacles in My Listening Process
- Getting distracted by mobile phone or surroundings.
- Thinking about my reply while the other person is talking.
- Losing focus during long discussions.
- Assuming I already know what the speaker is saying.
- Stress or tiredness affecting concentration.

## Question 4: What I Can Do to Improve My Listening
- Keep phone aside during conversations.
- Focus completely on the speaker.
- Take short notes in meetings.
- Ask questions to clarify doubts.
- Repeat important points to confirm understanding.
- Practice patience and avoid interrupting.

## Question 5: When I Switch to Passive Communication
- When talking to seniors or managers.
- When I want to avoid arguments.
- When I feel unsure about the topic.
- During formal meetings where I hesitate to speak.

## Question 6: When I Switch to Aggressive Communication
- When I am very stressed or frustrated.
- When deadlines are very tight and work is delayed.
- When I feel someone is not listening to my points.

## Question 7: When I Switch to Passive-Aggressive Communication
- When I feel upset but do not express it directly.
- When I use sarcasm instead of speaking openly.
- When I stay silent instead of explaining the problem.

## Question 8: How I Can Make My Communication Assertive
1. Speak clearly and confidently without fear.
2. Express my opinions politely and respectfully.
3. Use “I feel” or “I think” statements instead of blaming others.
4. Listen to others’ viewpoints before responding.
5. Stay calm even during disagreements.
6. Practice saying “no” politely when required.
